## Gemini for ALS: Precision Support for High-Intensity Needs

The Challenge: The Differentiation "Floor" In Alternative Learning Settings (ALS), the gap between standard grade-level materials and a student’s functional developmental level can be vast. Traditional differentiation often doesn't go "low" enough or provide the heavy visual and linguistic scaffolding required for our students to access the curriculum.

The Solution: Precision Scaffolding By using Gemini and NotebookLM, we can create custom, "grounded" materials that honor the age-appropriateness of the grade-level standards while meticulously adjusting the complexity to meet a student's specific IEP goals and cognitive profile.

Specialized Workflows for ALS

- Visual Schedule & Social Story Generation: Use Gemini to quickly draft scripts for Social Stories or descriptive text for visual schedules. By providing the AI with a student's specific "trigger" or a classroom routine, you can generate a personalized narrative in seconds.
- Radical Text Simplification: Standard "leveled" texts are often still too complex for elementary ALS learners. Use specific prompts to strip away non-essential language, focusing only on "Core Vocabulary" and simple Subject-Verb-Object sentence structures.
- Communication Partner Training: Create a Gemini Gem that acts as a "Communication Partner Coach" for paraeducators. The Gem provides instant ideas for Aided Language Stimulation (modeling on a student's AAC device) based on a specific lesson or activity.

Quality Control: The ALS Standard In ALS, the stakes for "Human-in-the-Loop" are at their highest.

- Developmental Check: AI often defaults to a "childish" tone when simplified. As the educator, you ensure the content remains age-respectful while being cognitively accessible.
- Sensory Considerations: When using AI to generate lesson hooks, the "Human Pilot" must ensure that suggestions (like videos or high-energy games) align with the sensory profiles and regulation needs of the students.

HCPSS Strategic Plan Alignment

This specialized instructional framework serves as the embodiment of district-wide commitment to our most vulnerable learner populations.

- Priority 1: Strengthen Learning and Instruction: This work directly fulfills the mandate for Outcome 1.1. By providing the "Precision Scaffolding" necessary for students with significant cognitive disabilities, we are operationalizing the district's promise to "tailor evidence-based interventions" for every student, regardless of their starting point.
- Priority 3: Foster Staff Growth & Engagement: Special education requires an immense amount of administrative and preparatory labor. By demonstrating how AI can produce high-quality, specialized materials in minutes rather than hours, we fulfill the promise of valuing staff time.

Strategic Impact: This efficiency ensures that our educators have the emotional and temporal bandwidth to focus on what matters most in an ALS setting: direct student connection, behavioral regulation, and relationship-building.
